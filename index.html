Artificial Intelligence

Overview

What do web search, speech recognition, face recognition, machine translation, autonomous driving, and automatic scheduling have in common? These are all complex real-world problems, and the goal of artificial intelligence (AI) is to tackle these with rigorous mathematical tools by creating intelligent machines, especially intelligent computer programs. It is related to the similar task of using computers to understand human intelligence, but AI does not have to confine itself to methods that are biologically observable.

Here are some of the typical problems where AI is applied:

  • Pattern recognition (optical character recognition, handwriting recognition, speech recognition, face recognition)
  • Artificial Creativity: to model, simulate or replicate creativity using a computer, in order to achieve goals such as constructing a program or computer capable of human-level creativity
  • Computer vision: science of computers that can see
  • Virtual reality/ Image processing
  • Diagnosis: development of algorithms and techniques that are able to determine whether the behaviour of a system is correct
  • Game theory: the study of mathematical models of conflict and cooperation between intelligent rational decision-makers.

Career Prospects

In recent years, Artificial Intelligence has turned from a niche area into mainstream computer science. Major companies such as Google, IBM, and Facebook are now seriously investing in AI research. Both Google and Facebook have recently bought several AI start-ups. It is expected that AI will be at the heart of new IT developments in areas like knowledge automation, self-driving cars, the Internet, and advanced robotics.

AI has become like wow in Silicon Valley today says Akli Adjaoute, founder and CEO of Brighterion, a software company that uses machine learning techniques to spot financial fraud for credit card customers.

Even though AI programmers are hirable with only bachelor's degrees, many pursue a master's degree or doctorate to get higher paying jobs. Because of the experience required for entry and upper-level positions, the average AI engineer can comfortably expect to make an annual salary of more than $90,000 (in the US). Here are some popular AI careers:

  • Robotic scientist: Graduate degree often needed. You will be responsible for creating robots and robotic systems that are able to perform duties that humans are either unable or prefer not to complete.
  • Face recognition software engineer: Many companies, including security companies, police departments, casinos, and even Google are utilizing face recognition to understand the people benefiting from their service.
  • Game programmer: Everybody loves video games, but newer games require highly intelligent enemies to keep players challenged.
